## What it is

*Mochi's Garden* is a cozy little platformer my youngest made — the idea, the
story, the cat, and the feel of it are all theirs; they built it by describing
what they wanted to Claude and shaping it from there. You play as **Mochi**, a
cat drifting through a dream made of **six small worlds**: *The Garden*, *The
Rooftops*, *The Treetops*, *The Snowy Cottage*, *Above the Clouds*, and *The
Starlit Dream*. In each one you gather every ball of **yarn** and make your way
to a cozy **basket** at the end. When the last world is crossed, Mochi curls
into the basket and the dream wraps soft around her. Naptime.

It's deliberately gentle: **arrow keys to move, space to jump**, with a
**double-jump** so nothing feels out of reach — and **falling is harmless**,
there's no way to lose. Just a soft, warm world to wander through.

## How it's built

The whole game is a **single self-contained HTML file** — no framework, no
libraries, no build step. The world is drawn on an **HTML5 `<canvas>`** with
plain **JavaScript**, and the menus, HUD, and the cream-and-rose storybook look
are styled with **CSS** (Google's *Fraunces* and *Nunito* fonts). That's the
entire stack, which is part of what makes it nice: it's small enough to read
top to bottom and runs anywhere a browser does.

Under the hood it's a classic **game loop**: every frame reads the keyboard,
nudges Mochi by gravity and momentum, checks her against the platforms and the
yarn, and redraws the scene. Each of the six worlds is just a little list of
platforms, yarn, and a basket — so adding a new world is mostly describing a new
layout.
